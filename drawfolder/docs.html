#Overview

VisionDraw enables direct, practical flows for hardware, education, documents, and teams with a modern, fast documentation‑first UI.

Pen, arrow, and shape tools let users mark regions, label components, and add notes directly on images for precise, context‑aware questions and answers.

Short answers, clear outcomes designed to minimize back‑and‑forth and accelerate troubleshooting or study.

#Quick start

  1. Open an image or the first page of a PDF.
  2. Use Pen, Arrow, Rectangle, or Ellipse to mark targets and add short labels.
  3. Write a targeted question referencing the marks or labels.
  4. Submit to receive an instant, context‑aware answer with the visual focus preserved.
Tip: Specific, localized questions yield faster and more reliable answers, especially on technical diagrams and receipts.

#Annotation tools

  • Pen: freehand emphasis or quick sketches.
  • Arrow: point to exact components, traces, or features.
  • Rectangle / Ellipse: frame regions of interest for clear reference.
  • Labels & notes: add brief descriptors, counts, or identifiers.
  • Circle connectors & highlight chips: chain related parts or steps for step‑wise explanations.
Designed for force diagrams, circuit annotations, PCB markings, and document line‑item highlights.

#Asking questions

  • Be explicit: reference shapes or labels (e.g., “What is the fault at arrow A?”).
  • Keep scope tight: one question per marked region improves precision.
  • Prefer concrete asks: “Compute total including tax” or “Identify missing component value.”
Targeted, annotated prompts reduce ambiguity and increase answer accuracy.

#Supported formats

  • Images: JPG, PNG, WebP.
  • PDF: first page is supported for fast annotation and extraction.
Optimized for screenshots, diagrams, receipts, and schematics.

#Use cases

  • Hardware: circle connectors, mark damage on PCBs, and label components for faster identification.
  • Education: annotate force diagrams or circuits for step‑wise explanations that students grasp quickly.
  • Documents: highlight totals, taxes, and line items for precise extractions with verification.
  • Teams: share annotated queries, comment, and track revisions across devices.
Built for clarity and speed across practical workflows.

#Accuracy & limits

Claim: Up to 95% on clear, annotated technical questions; accuracy depends on image quality and prompt clarity.

Note: Complex, low‑contrast, or multi‑question prompts may require iterative clarification.

Best results come from crisp annotations, unambiguous targets, and single‑intent queries that align with the marked regions.

#Privacy & security

  • Encrypted in transit and at rest.
  • No training on user data.
Enterprise posture prioritizes confidentiality for technical, academic, and financial documents.

#Workspaces & sharing

  • Shared workspaces for collaboration.
  • Comments and revision history for review workflows.
  • Availability in Pro/Enterprise tiers.
Consistent review history helps maintain auditability across teams and devices.

#Tips

  • Prefer arrows and tight frames to isolate the exact region of interest.
  • Keep labels short; use multiple marks rather than long paragraphs.
  • Ask one concrete question per submission for the fastest answer turnaround.

#FAQ

What content types work best? Clear technical images, diagrams, and receipts with distinct regions of interest.

Do PDFs work? Yes—first page is supported for quick annotation and extraction.

Is collaboration supported? Shared workspaces, comments, and revision history are available on Pro/Enterprise.

Any privacy guarantees? Data is encrypted in transit and at rest, and not used for training.
